Leveling Process (How To Level Your Character)

Leveling Process (How To Level Your Character)

We use D&D Beyond as the single source of truth for all characters:

https://www.dndbeyond.com/campaigns/7394979

All leveling must be completed there.

When Leveling Happens

  • Leveling is triggered by the DM (milestone system)
  • It will usually happen at the end of a session
  • If needed, players may stay briefly after session to complete updates

Step-by-Step Leveling Checklist

Follow these steps in order every time.

1. Open Your Character

  • Log into D&D Beyond
  • Open your character sheet
  • Click “Manage Levels”

2. Apply Your Level Up

  • Increase your level by 1
  • Select your class (or multiclass if applicable) (No multi classing yet)

3. Hit Points (Required)

Choose one:

  • Take the fixed value
  • OR roll at the table

House Rule:
If you roll a 1, you may re-roll once

4. Class Features (Required)

Carefully review everything gained at this level:

  • New abilities
  • Upgraded abilities
  • Resource increases (charges, uses, etc.)

Do not skip this.

👉 If you don’t understand something, ask.

5. Spellcasters Only

If your class uses spells:

Learn / Prepare Spells

  • Add new spells if your class allows it
  • Update prepared spells list if applicable

Check:

  • Spell slots increased?
  • Known spells increased?
  • Cantrips changed?

👉 Most leveling mistakes happen here.

6. Ability Score Improvement / Feat (If Applicable)

At certain levels you will choose:

  • +2 to one stat
  • OR +1 to two stats
  • OR a Feat

Take your time here. This is one of the most important decisions.

7. Proficiency Changes

Check for:

  • Proficiency bonus increase
  • New proficiencies (skills, tools, weapons)

Make sure they are reflected on your sheet.

8. Review Your Sheet (Critical Step)

Before you finish:

  • HP total correct?
  • Attack bonuses updated?
  • Spell save DC correct?
  • Features visible on your sheet?

If something looks wrong, fix it now.

9. Sync With the Table

Before next session:

  • Make sure your character is saved
  • Be ready to explain any new abilities briefly, we will do a brief leveling block at the beginning of the next session where we can talk about leveling and any questions before the recap from last session.

Fast Leveling Rule (Table Efficiency)

Leveling should not slow the game down.

If you are not finished:

  • You use your previous level abilities only
  • You may finalize between sessions

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Forgetting to increase HP
  • Missing new spells or spell slots
  • Not reading new abilities
  • Choosing feats without understanding them
  • Leaving your sheet half-updated

Table Standard

You are responsible for your character.

The DM will help, but:

  • You must complete your level
  • You must understand your abilities
  • You must come prepared

Simple Rule

If you cannot explain your new ability in one sentence…

You don’t understand it yet.
