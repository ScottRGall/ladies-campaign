House Rules

Player/Rules/Character Creation Rules

  1. The campaign will start at level 1 for everyone.
  2. Everyone uses Point Buy Player Point Buy Worksheet
  3. Everyone starts with 27 points.
  4. One ability should intentionally be weaker than the others.
  5. Full rebuilds are allowed until the start of Session 2. After this it will have to work narratively with DM approval after this. I'm not opposed to character changes but I'd like to avoid destabilizing the party in the process.

Starting Gold and Equipment – D&D Basic Rules

Should a new character need to join us in the middle of the campaign, we will most likely start that character at current player character levels unless the narrative and player/dm agree otherwise.

Healing Potion House Rules

Death and Consequences

This campaign uses standard D&D 5e rules for falling unconscious and death saving throws.

Character death is a real possibility. Choices matter, combat can be dangerous, and caution is sometimes the wiser path. Without meaningful consequences, there is no reason to be careful.

Resurrection magic exists in the world but follows normal rules. Time limits apply, required spells must be available, and material costs are in line with published rules. Not every death will be easily reversible, and resurrection is never guaranteed to be immediate or convenient.

If a character dies, we will handle it respectfully and collaboratively. Options may include resurrection if circumstances allow, introducing a new character, or weaving the loss into the story in a meaningful way.

The goal is not to punish players, but to ensure tension, stakes, and emotional weight remain part of the game.

Milestone Experience

Experience will be done through Milestones, I.E. when the DM says you level up... we level your characters up. This will typically be done at the tail end of a session so anyone who needs help doing so may stay after and get the help they need.

Flanking

We will use the optional flanking rules in combat.

When two allies are positioned on opposite sides of a creature and both can attack it, they gain advantage on melee attacks against that creature. This applies equally to player characters and enemies.

Positioning and movement matter. Enemies will use flanking when it makes sense, just as players can.

Leveling

When leveling players will have the choice to either take the default HP increase or roll their hit points at the table (Hit Point Increases in D&D 5e), should you roll a 1 you may re-roll once per level.

Alignment

Choose your Alignment carefully, I'd like to use these as a guide for how your character will react in situations

Racial Bonuses

Racial Bonuses will be used as written in the 5e Players handbook (Racial Ability Score Increases – D&D Basic Rules)

Encumbrance

We will not be strictly using encumbrance, but I will likely slow you down some if you start carrying around something which is obviously going to slow you down...

Critical Success

We will be using the formula (2 x dice) + mod.

Critical Failure

Will depending on narrative cause a fumble which may, harm you or someone else you didn't intend, drop a weapon... things like this. I won't be breaking weapons etc. on you.

Inspiration

The DM may award Inspiration for exceptional role playing, meaningful character driven choices, or moments that strongly reflect a character’s values.

Inspiration can be spent to gain advantage on a single roll and does not stack.

Skill Check Attempts

For most situations, up to two different characters may attempt the same skill check. Alternatively, one character may take the lead while another provides help, granting advantage on the roll.

For group checks, everyone involved will roll. The lowest roll is discarded (when 6+ players are rolling), and the majority result will generally determine the outcome, adjusted as needed for the narrative situation.

## Resting and Health Regeneration

We use standard 5e resting rules.

  • A short rest is at least 1 hour of downtime during which you can spend Hit Dice to regain hit points.
  • A long rest is at least 8 hours of rest including sleep. After a long rest you regain all hit points and some Hit Dice back. You can benefit from only one long rest per 24 hours.
    For full details see the official Basic Rules: https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/basic-rules-2014/adventuring#Resting
